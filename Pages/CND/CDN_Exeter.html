Exploring Infectious Disease Dynamics

A Brief Description:

This project involves the analysis of a simplified SIR (Susceptible-Infectious-Recovered) infectious disease model using MATLAB. The model is defined by nonlinear differential equations, focusing on the dynamics of susceptible (S) and infected (I) populations. By varying the infection rate parameter (β), I explored the number, stability, and behavior of steady states and periodic orbits. The project includes the implementation of key functions—MyJacobian, MySolve, and MyTrackCurve—to identify and track equilibria, along with plotting bifurcation diagrams to assess stability across different β values.
